Tekau ma rima Hakihea

December 15th

Dear rātaka,

Today started like any normal day. I climbed up my treehouse after wharekura carrying my schoolbag and a bag of chips I DID NOT want to share with my Tuakana. I did my wharekura homework calmly with the sun shining through the gaps in the wooden plank. Halfway through doing my Social Studies homework a brutal reminder hit me.

KIRIHIMETE IS ONLY 10 DAYS AWAY. TEN LIKE ONE ZERO. And guess what — I hadn't sorted out my gifts for anyone. Not māmā, not pāpā, not even my 2 tuakana.

At first I thought, "EH, it's fine, I'll just buy something." But then I checked the prices online. My jaw literally fell on the floor. 1 custom phone case is $15. FIFTEEN dollars! JUST TO GET SOMETHING PRINTED ON USING UV? WHAT IS WITH THIS INFLATION. ARGHHHHHHHHH. I can't even buy 1, I only had $14.60 in my wallet. 😭

I felt the familiar sinking feeling in my chest so very similar to the time I forgot to bring my social studies book to wharekura. I don't want to be that one kid who doesn't give nothing. I want to give something personal to each person. But right now, I'm stuck. I'm gonna go to sleep now.😴Fingers crossed! 🤞

Tekau ma ono Hakihea

December 16th

Dear rātaka,

So I thought of a bloody brilliant idea. I could just MAKE the gifts. Ecstatically, I spent most of today searching for ideas. I first looked for cardboard in the garage but there wasn't any left. I went online to search for "DIY Kirhimete Crafts." 🎄

Unfortunately, Google didn't come up with an AI overview and I'm too ngenge to do the traditional research. 🦥🗿

I still don't know what I'm making but at least I know what to do. It's a step forward I guess. For now. 😑

Tekau ma iwa Hakihea

December 19th

Dear rātaka,

I FOUND THE IDEA!!!🔥🔥🔥🔥🔥🔥🔥🔥🔥

I was doomscrolling on Youtube and this 5-minute crafts video popped up (I don't know how it got into my feed probably because I binge watch Peppa Pig). It was a video of some sort of "memory box". It was just a simple, personalised, well decorated box where you store special, memorable stuff like notes, mounga, and photos of memorable moments.

I can make one for my mama, papa, and taukana! Each one different, each one meaning something special to them.

I felt so relieved that I finally thought of the idea. I AM A GENIUS. AN ABSOLUTE GENIUS.

Rua tekau Hakihea

December 20th

Dear rātaka,

Today was construction day and it was messy — I got glue ALL over my hands but it was pretty awesome I guess.

I cut the stiff, hard pepamārō into pieces and glued them together. The first one looked like a 3D rhombus 😭🙏. The second was a bit better. The third one? It was just so good. It was Simply Lovely, SIMPLY FREAKING BLOODY LOVELY. 😎

Even though my fingers got fused together and I somehow got glue on my nose. I don't know how that happened. But surprisingly, this was probably the only time I actually felt happy crafting.

The boxes are actually coming together pretty well, and I am pretty happy with it. 😁

December 21st

Dear rātaka,

Today I was making the box aesthetically pleasing ✨. I got glitter everywhere and the paint got the glitter stuck to my clothes (mom will not be very happy🥹🙏).

I can't believe I actually managed to be able to craft these. BY MYSELF.

25th December

CHRISTMAS DAY!

Dear Diary,

BEST. KIRIHIMETE. EVER!

Everyone opened their presents and Mum cried happy tears. Dad said, "This is better than anything I can get from any shop.". My brother hugged (more like tackled me) so hard I fell onto the ground.

Turns out it's really not about money but all about time, effort, a whole lot of heart, and a lot of wasted glue and dirty clothes.

I felt so proud and this reminded me of a Maori proverb:

"Ahakoa he iti he pounamu"
Although it is small, it is greenstone
